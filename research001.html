Clustering the Quality of Indonesian Youth 2023 for Effective Empowerment

In 2023, the number of youth in Indonesia aged 16-30 reached 64.16 million, or approximately 23.18% of the total population, with the highest percentage in Papua (26.64%) and the lowest in East Java (21.09%). Youth are considered the nation's asset due to their roles in social control, moral strength, and as agents of change in national development. However, youth empowerment faces challenges in meeting basic needs such as education, livability, and employment opportunities. The Ministry of Youth and Sports measures youth development through the Youth Development Index (YDI), which includes aspects of education, health, and socio-economic conditions. This study aims to cluster provinces in Indonesia based on youth quality in these aspects using partition clustering methods, namely k-means and k-medoids, to assist the government in planning effective and inclusive youth development policies.

Thematic Map of Clustering Results

The first cluster shows strong education with high schooling years and good internet access, and the highest participation in formal and non-formal training. However, it faces health challenges, including high early marriage and low birth weight babies. Despite low youth sickness rates, unemployment is low, and many youth have bank accounts and live in decent housing. Improvement should focus on formal education and health access.

The second cluster has good education and internet access but lower participation in training, as youth tend to learn independently online. Early marriage and low birth weight babies are moderate, and health insurance coverage is low. Unemployment is the highest, and although many youth have bank accounts and decent housing, health access needs improvement.

The third cluster excels in education and health, with high schooling years, good internet access, and active participation in training. Early marriage and low birth weight rates are low, and health insurance coverage is high, but youth sickness rates are also high. Despite high savings, many youth live in inadequate housing. The focus should be on expanding job opportunities and improving health and housing.

The fourth cluster, Papua, lags in education, health, and socio-economics. Youth have low schooling years, limited internet access, and high early marriage and low birth weight rates. While sickness rates are low, unemployment is the lowest due to informal labor. Housing conditions are poor, and improving education and infrastructure is crucial.
